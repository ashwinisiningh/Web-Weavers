1) HISTORICAL MONUMENTS

BADA IMAMBARA

The Bara Imambara of Lucknow is one of the most famous monuments in the city. The Imambara is primarily known for its incredible maze, known as Bhul Bhulaiya locally, which is located on the upper floor of the monument. It is said that there are 1024 ways to get inside the maze but only 2 to come out.

THE RESIDENCY

The Residency, also called as the British Residency and Residency Complex, is a group of several buildings in a common precinct in the city of Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India. It served as the residence for the British Resident General who was a representative in the court of the Nawab

CHOTA IMAMBARA

It is also known as the Hussainabad Imambara and was built in 1837 to 1842.This Imambara is known for its wonderfully gilded dome and the turrets here are an exquisite example of craftsmanship. It was built by Muhammad Ali Shah . It is one of the most pieces of architecture that any Nawab has built in India so be sure not to miss it.

Chattar Manzil

Chattar Manzil is a historic building in Lucknow. It was started by Nawab Ghaziuddin Haider of Awadh and after his death his successor, Nawab Naseeruddin Haider, completed it. The main room of this double-storey building is of double standards and on it there is a huge gold umbrella which can be seen from a distance.

Hussainabad Clock Tower

The renowned Hussainabad Clock Tower is another popular tourist spot of Lucknow with its rich heritage and intricate beauty.The Hussainabad neighbourhood of Lucknow is home to the historic Hussainabad Clock Tower.The highest clock tower in India, also known as Ghantaghar, is one of the most popular tourist spots in Lucknow.

RUMI DARWAZA

The Rumi Darwaza, in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India, is an imposing gateway which was built by Nawab Asaf-Ud-Daula in 1784. It is an example of Awadhi architecture. The Rumi Darwaza, which stands sixty feet tall, was modeled after the Sublime Porte in Istanbul
