Bengaluru

Bengaluru, also known as Bangalore, is the capital of the Indian state of Karnataka. It is often referred to as the "Silicon Valley of India" due to its prominent IT and technology industry. Bengaluru is a city of contrasts, combining modern skyscrapers with historical landmarks and lush green spaces.

Hyderabad

Hyderabad is the capital of the Indian state of Telangana and is known for its rich history, including the Qutb Shahi and Nizam dynasties. The city is famous for its delicious biryani and the iconic Charminar monument.

Chennai

Chennai, formerly known as Madras, is the capital of the state of Tamil Nadu. It is a city with a rich cultural heritage and is known for its classical music, dance, and historical landmarks like Marina Beach and Fort St. George.

Kolkata

Kolkata, the capital of the state of West Bengal, has a significant place in India's history. It was the capital of British India until 1911 and is known for its intellectual and cultural contributions. The city is famous for the Howrah Bridge and the Victoria Memorial.

Jaipur

Jaipur is the capital of the Indian state of Rajasthan and is known for its rich history, colorful architecture, and vibrant culture. The city is often referred to as the "Pink City" due to its distinctive pink-colored buildings.

Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad is the largest city in the state of Gujarat and is renowned for its association with Mahatma Gandhi. The Sabarmati Ashram, a place closely associated with Gandhi's life, is located in Ahmedabad.

Pune

Pune, in the state of Maharashtra, is known for its educational institutions and historical significance. It is often referred to as the "Oxford of the East" due to the presence of several prestigious colleges and universities.

Lucknow

Lucknow, the capital of the state of Uttar Pradesh, has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its Nawabi culture, historical monuments, and delicious cuisine, including kebabs and biryani.

Varanasi

Varanasi, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, is a spiritual and cultural hub on the banks of the Ganges River. It is a pilgrimage site for Hindus and is known for its ghats and temples.

Amritsar

Amritsar is a city in the state of Punjab and is known for its iconic Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib), which is a sacred site for Sikhs. The city is also famous for its vibrant culture and cuisine.

Chandigarh

Chandigarh is a well-planned city and serves as the capital of both Punjab and Haryana. It is known for its modern architecture and urban design, and it was designed by the renowned architect Le Corbusier.

Goa

Goa is a coastal state known for its beautiful beaches, rich history, and vibrant nightlife. It was a Portuguese colony for centuries and has a unique blend of Indian and Western cultures.

Patna

Patna, the capital of the state of Bihar, has a significant historical legacy. It was a center of learning and culture in ancient India and is associated with several historical figures and events.

Rajkot

Rajkot is a city in the state of Gujarat and is known for its traditional handicrafts, jewelry, and silk embroidery. It is also the birthplace of Mahatma Gandhi.

Guwahati

Guwahati is the largest city in the Indian state of Assam and is known for its scenic beauty, ancient temples, and cultural significance. The Kamakhya Temple is one of the most prominent religious sites in the city.

Nagpur

Nagpur is the winter capital of the state of Maharashtra and is famous for its oranges. The city is also known for its historical landmarks and is often called the "Tiger Capital of India" due to its proximity to several tiger reserves.

Thiruvananthapuram

Thiruvananthapuram, the capital of the state of Kerala, is known for its beautiful beaches, historic palaces, and cultural heritage. The city has a tropical climate and is a popular tourist destination.

Indore

Indore, in the state of Madhya Pradesh, is known for its culinary delights and is often called the "Food Capital of India." The city also boasts historical sites and a thriving business and trade environment.

Ludhiana

Ludhiana, in the state of Punjab, is an industrial city known for its manufacturing and textile industries. It is also a cultural hub with a rich tradition of music, dance, and festivals.

Vadodara

Vadodara, also known as Baroda, is a city in the state of Gujarat and is known for its palaces, museums, and educational institutions. The city has a rich history and is a center for art and culture.

Raipur

Raipur is the capital of the state of Chhattisgarh and is known for its rapidly developing economy and infrastructure. It has a mix of modern and traditional elements, making it a diverse and vibrant city.

Visakhapatnam

Visakhapatnam, often referred to as Vizag, is a coastal city in Andhra Pradesh. It is known for its natural beauty, beaches, and a growing information technology sector.

Kanpur

Kanpur, in the state of Uttar Pradesh, has a rich industrial history and is famous for its leather and textile industries. It also has several historical sites and educational institutions.

Mysuru

Mysuru, also known as Mysore, is a city in the state of Karnataka known for its royal heritage, beautiful palaces, and the famous Mysore Dasara festival.

Kochi

Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a coastal city in the state of Kerala. It is known for its picturesque backwaters, historic Fort Kochi, and a diverse cultural heritage influenced by various foreign traders and settlers.

Srinagar

Srinagar is the summer capital of the union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. It is famous for its beautiful Dal Lake, houseboats, and Mughal gardens. The city offers a serene and scenic environment.

Aurangabad

Aurangabad is a city in the state of Maharashtra and is known for its historical and cultural significance. It is home to the famous Ajanta and Ellora Caves, UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Surat

Surat is a city in the state of Gujarat and is known for its diamond and textile industries. It is a major business and trade hub and has a rich culinary heritage.

Coimbatore

Coimbatore is a city in the state of Tamil Nadu and is an industrial and educational center in South India. It is known for its engineering and textile industries and is often called the "Manchester of South India."

Jodhpur

Jodhpur, in the state of Rajasthan, is known as the "Blue City" due to the blue-painted buildings in the old part of the city. It is famous for the majestic Mehrangarh Fort and its cultural heritage.

Madurai

Madurai, one of the oldest cities in India, is known for the Meenakshi Amman Temple, a historic and architectural marvel. The city has a rich cultural and religious heritage.

Nashik

Nashik is a city in Maharashtra and is known for its association with the Hindu epic Ramayana. It is a major pilgrimage site, and the Kumbh Mela, a major festival, is held here.

Meerut

Meerut is a city in the state of Uttar Pradesh and is known for its historical significance. It played a significant role in the Indian Rebellion of 1857 against British colonial rule.

Faridabad

Faridabad is a city in the state of Haryana and is known for its industrial and manufacturing sectors. It is part of the National Capital Region (NCR) and has a growing economy.

Jamshedpur

Jamshedpur is a planned city in the state of Jharkhand and is known for its steel industry. It was founded by the visionary industrialist Jamsetji Tata and is often called the "Steel City."

Kota

Kota, in the state of Rajasthan, is a major coaching center for competitive exams. It is known for its educational institutions and has earned the nickname "Education City."

Kollam

Kollam, also known as Quilon, is a city in the state of Kerala. It is known for its historic port and cashew processing industry. Kollam is a gateway to the famous backwaters of Kerala.

Ranchi

Ranchi is the capital of the state of Jharkhand and is known for its scenic beauty and natural resources. The city is surrounded by lush greenery and hills, making it a picturesque destination.

Alappuzha

Alappuzha, often called Alleppey, is a city in Kerala known for its intricate network of backwaters and serene houseboat rides. It is a popular destination for nature lovers and tourists.

Mangalore

Mangalore is a coastal city in the state of Karnataka and is known for its beautiful beaches, temples, and vibrant culture. The city is also a major port on the Arabian Sea.

Thrissur

Thrissur, often referred to as the "Cultural Capital of Kerala," is known for its cultural festivals and heritage. The city is famous for the Thrissur Pooram festival and historic temples.

Gwalior

Gwalior is a city in the state of Madhya Pradesh and is known for its historic Gwalior Fort, which has a rich history and architectural significance. The city is also known for its classical music tradition.

Amravati

Amravati is a city in Maharashtra and is known for its historical temples, educational institutions, and cultural heritage. It is a blend of tradition and modernity.

Kollam

Kollam, also known as Quilon, is a city in the state of Kerala. It is known for its historic port and cashew processing industry. Kollam is a gateway to the famous backwaters of Kerala.

Rourkela

Rourkela is a planned city located in the state of Odisha. It is known for its steel plant, educational institutions, and a diverse culture. The city has a mix of urban and natural attractions.

Durgapur

Durgapur is an industrial city in the state of West Bengal. It is known for its steel and alloy industries and has a well-planned layout. The city offers a blend of modernity and tradition.

Warangal

Warangal is a historical city in the Indian state of Telangana. It was once the capital of the Kakatiya Dynasty and is known for its ancient temples, including the Thousand Pillar Temple.

Guntur

Guntur is a city in the state of Andhra Pradesh and is known for its agriculture and educational institutions. The city has a rich cultural heritage and is famous for its spicy cuisine.

Kapurthala

Kapurthala is a city in the state of Punjab and is known for its historical and architectural landmarks. The city's architecture is influenced by both French and Indo-Saracenic styles.

Saharanpur

Saharanpur is a city in the state of Uttar Pradesh and is known for its woodworking industry, particularly the crafting of wooden furniture and artifacts.

Mathura

Mathura is a city in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh and is revered as the birthplace of Lord Krishna. The city has numerous temples, including the Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple.

Bilaspur

Bilaspur is a city in the state of Chhattisgarh and is known for its cultural festivals, unique cuisine, and the historic Chakradhar Samaroh festival.

Gaya

Gaya is a city in the state of Bihar and is a major religious center for Buddhists. It is home to the Mahabodhi Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Tirunelveli

Tirunelveli is a city in the state of Tamil Nadu and is known for its rich cultural heritage and distinctive cuisine. The city has historical temples and a vibrant arts scene.

Ujjain

Ujjain is an ancient city in the state of Madhya Pradesh and is considered one of the seven holiest cities in Hinduism. It is known for the Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga temple and the Kumbh Mela.

Dehradun

Dehradun is the capital city of the Indian state of Uttarakhand. It is known for its picturesque landscapes, educational institutions, and pleasant climate. The city is a gateway to the Himalayas.

Dhanbad

Dhanbad is a city in the state of Jharkhand and is often referred to as the "Coal Capital of India." It has a significant coal mining industry and is an industrial hub.

Raichur

Raichur is a city in the Indian state of Karnataka and is known for its historical sites, including the Raichur Fort and the Ek Minar Ki Masjid. It has a rich cultural heritage.

Kakinada

Kakinada is a coastal city in the state of Andhra Pradesh and is known for its scenic beaches, thriving port, and seafood. The city has a maritime and cultural heritage.

Belagavi

Belagavi, also known as Belgaum, is a city in the state of Karnataka. It is known for its lush greenery, historic sites, and cultural diversity. The city has a blend of Kannada and Marathi cultures.

Ajmer

Ajmer is a city in the state of Rajasthan and is famous for the Ajmer Sharif Dargah, a Sufi shrine. It has a rich historical and religious significance and is a major pilgrimage site.

Gulbarga

Gulbarga, now known as Kalaburagi, is a city in Karnataka and is known for its historical monuments, including the Gulbarga Fort and the Sharana Basaveshwara Temple.

Panipat

Panipat is a historic city in Haryana and is known for its role in three significant battles of Indian history. The city has a rich historical legacy and is known for its textiles and carpets.

Nizamabad

Nizamabad is a city in the Indian state of Telangana. It is known for its cultural diversity, historic sites, and agricultural activities. The city is named after the Nizams of Hyderabad.

Kollam

Kollam, also known as Quilon, is a city in the state of Kerala. It is known for its historic port and cashew processing industry. Kollam is a gateway to the famous backwaters of Kerala.

Rajahmundry

Rajahmundry is a city in Andhra Pradesh and is known for its cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and the Godavari River. It is often referred to as the "Cultural Capital of Andhra Pradesh."

Bikaner

Bikaner is a city in the state of Rajasthan and is known for its forts, palaces, and unique sweets. The city's Junagarh Fort is a prominent historical site.

Bharatpur

Bharatpur is a city in the state of Rajasthan and is famous for the Keoladeo National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is a significant bird sanctuary and attracts birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.

Dhar
